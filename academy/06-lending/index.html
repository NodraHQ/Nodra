01 — Overview

Lending

On-chain lending protocols let anyone borrow or lend assets without a credit check — because borrowers post collateral worth more than what they borrow, and that collateral can be automatically liquidated if its value falls.

This module will teach you how collateralization ratios work, how interest rates adjust with supply and demand, and exactly what happens — step by step — when a position gets liquidated.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • Why on-chain lending needs over-collateralization instead of a credit score.
  • How interest rate models respond to supply and demand in real time.
  • What loan-to-value (LTV) means and why it determines your safety margin.
  • What actually happens during a liquidation, and who profits from it.
  • How to monitor a borrowing position's health factor.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

Collateral
An asset locked up to secure a loan, which can be seized if the loan isn't repaid.
LTV
Loan-to-Value — the ratio between what you borrow and the value of your collateral.
Liquidation
The forced sale of collateral when a loan's safety margin falls too low.
Interest Rate Model
The formula a lending protocol uses to set borrowing and supply rates.
Health Factor
A live score representing how close a borrowing position is to liquidation.
