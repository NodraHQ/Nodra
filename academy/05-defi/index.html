01 — Overview

DeFi Fundamentals

Decentralized finance (DeFi) rebuilds services banks and exchanges normally provide — trading, lending, earning interest — as open protocols that anyone can use or build on, without a company custodying the funds.

This module will teach you the core primitive behind almost every DeFi protocol: the automated market maker, how liquidity pools replace order books, and the real risks — like impermanent loss — that come with providing liquidity.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • What problem DeFi actually solves compared to a traditional exchange.
  • How an automated market maker (AMM) prices assets without an order book.
  • What a liquidity pool is, and how liquidity providers earn fees.
  • What impermanent loss is, and why it's a real cost, not just a risk.
  • How to evaluate a DeFi protocol before using it.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

AMM
Automated Market Maker — a protocol that prices and swaps assets algorithmically, without an order book.
Liquidity Pool
A pool of two or more assets locked in a smart contract, used to enable trading.
Slippage
The difference between a trade's expected price and its actual executed price.
DEX
Decentralized Exchange — a trading protocol that runs without a central operator.
Impermanent Loss
The temporary loss liquidity providers can face when pooled asset prices diverge.
