01 — Overview

Security

The overwhelming majority of losses in crypto come from a small set of repeated patterns: phishing, contract exploits, and avoidable operational mistakes — not exotic, unstoppable attacks.

This module will teach you to recognize phishing attempts, understand what a smart contract audit does and doesn't guarantee, and build the personal security habits that remove most realistic risk.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • The most common phishing patterns used against wallet holders.
  • What kinds of bugs smart contract exploits typically take advantage of.
  • What an audit actually verifies — and its real limitations.
  • How rug pulls are structured, and the warning signs that precede them.
  • The personal security habits (opsec) that remove most realistic risk.
03 — Prerequisites

Prerequisites

  • Completed Module 02 — Wallets.
  • Familiarity with the DeFi track (Modules 05–08) is recommended.
04 — Glossary

Glossary

Audit
An independent review of a smart contract's code, looking for bugs and vulnerabilities.
Exploit
An attack that takes advantage of a specific bug or design flaw to steal or misdirect funds.
Rug Pull
A scam where a project's creators withdraw all liquidity or funds, abandoning users.
Reentrancy
A common smart contract bug where a function is re-entered before its state is updated.
Opsec
Operational security — the personal habits that reduce your exposure to attacks.
