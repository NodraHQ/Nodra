01 — Overview

Networks

Bitcoin proved one blockchain could work. Since then, hundreds of networks have launched, each making different trade-offs between speed, cost, and decentralization — Ethereum, Solana, and BNB Chain among the most used.

This module will teach you how to compare networks on their own terms: what a Layer 1 actually is, how transactions get confirmed, what gas fees pay for, and how assets move from one chain to another through bridges.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • How Ethereum, Solana, and BNB Chain differ in design and trade-offs.
  • What gas fees are actually paying for, and why they change.
  • How cross-chain bridges move assets between networks — and their risks.
  • What "finality" means and why it matters before you consider a transaction safe.
  • How to choose the right network for a given use case.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

Layer 1
A base blockchain network — like Ethereum or Solana — that handles its own consensus and security directly.
RPC
The endpoint a wallet or app uses to read from and send transactions to a blockchain network.
Gas
The fee paid to the network for processing a transaction or executing a smart contract.
Bridge
A protocol that moves assets or data from one blockchain network to another.
Finality
The point at which a transaction is considered permanently and irreversibly confirmed.
