01 — Overview

Stablecoins

A stablecoin is a token designed to hold a steady value, usually pegged to a currency like the US dollar. How that peg is maintained differs enormously between designs — and that difference is exactly where the risk lives.

This module will teach you to tell fiat-backed, crypto-backed, and algorithmic stablecoins apart, understand what "reserve attestation" actually proves, and recognize the warning signs that precede a depeg.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • The three main stablecoin designs: fiat-backed, crypto-backed, and algorithmic.
  • What a reserve attestation does — and doesn't — prove about a stablecoin.
  • Why over-collateralization is used to absorb crypto price volatility.
  • What a "depeg" is, and historical examples of why it happened.
  • How to evaluate a stablecoin's real backing before relying on it.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

Peg
The target value a stablecoin is designed to maintain, usually a fiat currency.
Collateralization Ratio
The value of backing assets held relative to the stablecoins issued.
Depeg
An event where a stablecoin's market price moves meaningfully away from its target peg.
Reserve Attestation
A report (not a full audit) confirming a stablecoin issuer's reserves at a point in time.
Algorithmic Stablecoin
A stablecoin that uses code and incentives, rather than collateral, to hold its peg.
