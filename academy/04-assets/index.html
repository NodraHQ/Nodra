01 — Overview

Assets

"Crypto" is not one thing. A native coin like ETH, a token built on top of it, a stablecoin, and an NFT are all fundamentally different kinds of on-chain assets, with different rules for how they're created and transferred.

This module will teach you to read a token's standard, tell fungible from non-fungible assets, understand what backs a stablecoin, and see how real-world assets (like real estate or bonds) get represented on-chain.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • The difference between a native coin and a token built on top of a network.
  • What token standards like ERC-20 and ERC-721 actually define.
  • Fungible vs. non-fungible assets, and why NFTs work differently from coins.
  • How stablecoins attempt to hold a steady value.
  • What a "real-world asset" (RWA) is when it's represented on-chain.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

Token Standard
A shared technical specification (like ERC-20) that lets wallets and apps handle a token predictably.
NFT
A non-fungible token — a unique, non-interchangeable digital asset recorded on-chain.
RWA
A real-world asset represented on a blockchain, such as tokenized real estate or bonds.
Fungibility
The property of being interchangeable — one unit is identical in value to any other.
Mint
The act of creating a new unit of a token or NFT on-chain.
