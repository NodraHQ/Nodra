01 — Overview

Yield

"Yield" in DeFi can come from very different places: securing a network through staking, earning trading fees by providing liquidity, or simply being paid in a protocol's own token to attract early users.

This module will teach you to separate these sources, understand why advertised APYs can be misleading, and recognize the signs of yield that exists only to be unsustainable.

02 — What You'll Learn

What You'll Learn

  • The difference between staking rewards, trading fees, and token emissions.
  • Why APY and APR are calculated differently, and why that matters.
  • How to read Total Value Locked (TVL) as a signal, not a guarantee.
  • Why very high advertised yields are usually a warning sign, not a bonus.
  • How to evaluate whether a yield source can realistically continue.

04 — Glossary

Glossary

APY
Annual Percentage Yield — a return rate that accounts for compounding.
Staking
Locking an asset to help secure a network (or protocol) in exchange for rewards.
Yield Farming
Moving capital between DeFi protocols to chase the highest available return.
Emissions
New tokens a protocol distributes as rewards, often to attract early liquidity.
TVL
Total Value Locked — the total value of assets deposited in a protocol.
